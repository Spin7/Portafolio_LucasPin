Smart Mechatronic Trap

In Progress 2024 – 2026

Overview

An IoT-connected mechatronic trap designed for the automatic capture, detection, and sex classification of Aedes aegypti mosquitoes. The system integrates embedded computer vision (YOLO-based models) with custom hardware to enable real-time, field-deployable mosquito monitoring — part of the broader Smart Mosquito Monitoring System thesis at FIUNA.

System Architecture

The trap is composed of integrated hardware and software modules that work together to autonomously capture, image, and classify mosquitoes in field conditions.

Vision Module

High-resolution camera with controlled lighting for consistent mosquito imaging inside the capture chamber, feeding frames to the on-device inference pipeline.

Edge Inference

YOLO-based detection and sex-classification models optimized via Knowledge Distillation to run in real-time on a Raspberry Pi embedded in the trap.

Capture Mechanism

Custom 3D-printed housing with an electromechanical system that directs attracted mosquitoes into the imaging chamber without damaging specimens.

IoT Connectivity

Wireless transmission of detection counts, sex-ratio, and event images to a central dashboard for remote monitoring and population tracking.

Power Management

Low-power design targeting field autonomy with battery operation and optional solar charging for extended outdoor deployments.

Data Pipeline

Structured logging of detection events with timestamps, GPS coordinates, and classification confidence for downstream epidemiological analysis.
