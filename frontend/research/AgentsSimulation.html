Agent-Based Simulation

Overview

An Agent-Based Model (ABM) that simulates Aedes aegypti mosquito population dynamics on a discretized urban grid. The model encodes the mosquito life cycle — egg, larva, pupa, and adult stages — and their interactions with environmental variables (temperature, rainfall, breeding sites) to study and project the effectiveness of vector control strategies such as fumigation and larvicide application.

Model Architecture

The simulation is built on a layered ABM architecture that models agent behavior, environmental coupling, and spatial dynamics simultaneously.

Urban Grid Environment

Discretized city-block grid where each cell encodes land-use type, breeding-site density, and local environmental conditions drawn from historical climate data.

Mosquito Agents

Individual agents progress through egg, larva, pupa, and adult life stages. Survival, development rates, and movement rules are parameterized from entomological literature.

Environmental Coupling

Temperature and rainfall time series drive stage-specific survival and development rates, enabling the model to replicate seasonal population peaks observed in field data.

Control Strategy Module

Configurable intervention scenarios (fumigation campaigns, larvicide schedules, site-elimination) that can be applied spatially and temporally to evaluate their impact.

Spatio-Temporal Analysis

Post-simulation analysis of population density maps, propagation fronts, and time-series to identify high-risk zones and optimal intervention windows.

Sensitivity Analysis

Systematic exploration of parameter space to quantify uncertainty and identify which environmental or behavioral parameters most influence population dynamics.

Research Progress

Milestones achieved and upcoming steps in the development of the Agent-Based Simulation.

2024 — Q1

Literature Review & Model Design Done

Reviewed entomological and ABM literature on Aedes aegypti population dynamics. Defined agent rules, environment structure, and parameterization strategy.

2024 — Q2

Baseline Model Implementation Done

Built initial ABM in Python (Mesa framework) with full mosquito life-cycle agents on a grid environment, temperature-driven development, and basic spatial dynamics.

2025 — Q3

CNMAC 2025 Poster & SCP Oral Presentation Done

Presented spatio-temporal analysis results at CNMAC 2025 (Rio de Janeiro) and the X Congress of Researchers of the SCP (Asunción), receiving peer feedback that informed model refinement.
